About Us

Everyone wants to know what will happen to interest rates, even if it's for very different reasons: finance people (duh!), buying a car, a house, or paying off that extra round of online shopping on your credit card.

And yes, interest rates seem to change for VERY random reasons. But maybe it's not always THAT random. For instance, the central bank (a.k.a The Fed) changes baseline interest rates every one or two months. When the Fed changes the interest rates, banks change theirs in tandem (yes, your bank, too!).

Good thing is, when they set the interest rate, the Fed people let us know what they're thinking before hand - and we think there might be significance to the specific words they use.

Our prediction models seem to think so, too! We used machine learning models to analyze and make predictions using the Fed's press releases. The two models we use specifically are decision trees, a predictive modeling approach, and neural networks, which are algorithms that mimic the way your brain actually learns.
